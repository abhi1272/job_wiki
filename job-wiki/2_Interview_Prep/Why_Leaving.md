## 🚪 Question: "Why are you looking to leave Concentrix?"

### Script:
"I have had a great run at Concentrix over the past 2.5 years. I had the opportunity to build our Enterprise AI Gateway from scratch, lead a team of 8 engineers to build our No-Code Agent Builder, and help modernize our GKE cloud infrastructure.

However, I've reached a point where the core AI platform is now stable and has transitioned into maintenance mode. I'm looking for my next challenge where I can design new, high-scale platforms, work on complex distributed systems, and continue pushing the boundaries of what full-stack engineering and GenAI systems can deliver. I want to bring my experience in microservices, cloud operations, and LLM orchestration to a product-focused company where platform scaling is a core engineering priority."

## 🎯 Question: "Why do you want to join our company?"

### Guidelines for Customization:
*Before the interview, research the target company and replace the bracketed sections below with their specific details.*

### Script:
"I want to join your company for three key reasons:

1. **Alignment with My Tech Stack & Scale:** You are building high-throughput microservices using [Node.js, React, and Kubernetes/GCP - match their stack] at a scale that presents interesting systems challenges. My experience scaling ITBoost's database lock limits at ConnectWise and managing containerized workloads on GKE fits this environment.
2. **Commitment to AI Platform Engineering:** I've read about [mention a recent product release, e.g. their new chat agent, automated workflow, or developer tooling]. Having spent the last 2 years building multi-agent systems and cost-routing gateways from the ground up, I understand the challenges of deploying these systems in production. I'm excited to apply my patterns (like hybrid RAG search, cost tracing, rate limiting) to your products.
3. **Engineering Culture:** I value team cultures that prioritize clean code design, comprehensive code reviews, and automated verification (like CI/CD pipelines). From what I've researched, your team maintains high engineering standards, and I want to contribute to and grow within that environment."
